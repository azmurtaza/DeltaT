# Code of conduct

DeltaT is a small project. Most of what happens here is people reporting odd
temperature readings, arguing about thermal physics, and occasionally telling me
the score is wrong. That is all welcome. This document exists so that everyone
knows where the line is, and so that I have something to point at on the rare
occasion someone crosses it.

## What is expected

Assume the other person is trying to help. Most disagreements here are technical,
and technical disagreements are the point of a project like this. Be direct about
the code and considerate about the person writing it.

Concretely:

- Criticise the measurement, the reasoning, or the code. Not the person.
- Accept that people arrive with different hardware, different experience levels,
  and different first languages. A question that looks obvious to you was not
  obvious to whoever asked it.
- If you are wrong, say so and move on. Nobody here is keeping score.
- If someone is wrong, correct them once, clearly, without an audience being made
  of it.

## What is not acceptable

- Harassment of any kind, public or private.
- Insults, personal attacks, or sustained hostility toward a contributor.
- Comments demeaning someone for their gender, sexual orientation, disability,
  physical appearance, body size, race, ethnicity, age, religion, or nationality.
- Sexual language or imagery, or unwelcome sexual attention.
- Publishing someone's private information without their explicit permission.
  This includes anything that shows up in a diagnostic dump: coordinates,
  machine names, serial numbers. Redact before you paste.
- Deliberately derailing issues, or repeatedly reopening a settled decision to
  wear people down.

## Where this applies

Anywhere the project happens: issues, pull requests, discussions, commit
messages, code review, and the in app feedback reports. It also applies when you
are representing DeltaT somewhere else, such as posting about it on Reddit or
answering questions about it in another community.

## Reporting

Email **azanmurtaza05@gmail.com** with what happened and where. If it is visible
publicly, a link is enough. If it is not, tell me what you can.

I will read it, keep it confidential, and tell you what I decided. I am one
person in one timezone, so I cannot promise a fast response, only an honest one.

If your report is about me, and you would rather not send it to me, say so
publicly in an issue or raise it with Open Source Collective, which hosts this
project's funding. I would rather you had somewhere to go than stay quiet.

## What happens next

Depending on what happened and whether it is a pattern, I will do one of:

1. **Say something privately.** A quiet word that a comment landed badly. This
   covers most of what ever actually comes up.
2. **A public correction.** When it happened in public and letting it stand would
   suggest it was fine.
3. **A temporary block.** Time away from the repository, with the reason stated.
4. **A permanent ban.** For harassment, for targeting someone, or for coming back
   and doing it again after being asked not to.

I will explain which one and why. If you think I got it wrong, tell me, and I
will look again.

## A note on the technical arguments

Nothing in here is meant to make people cautious about disagreeing. DeltaT makes
claims about physics that are checked against real measurements, and it has been
wrong before and will be wrong again. Telling me a number is wrong, bluntly, with
evidence, is a contribution and not a conduct problem. The distinction is between
"this is wrong and here is why" and "you are an idiot". Only the second one is a
problem.
